# Content Calendar

Design and maintain editorial content calendars — topic planning, publishing
cadence, seasonal alignment, and cross-platform coordination — ensuring
consistent, strategic content output.

## What problem this solves
Haphazard publishing leads to content gaps, topic repetition, and missed
seasonal opportunities. A content calendar plans topics weeks ahead, balances
content pillars, and aligns publishing with business events and audience needs.

## When to use
When planning content strategy. At the start of each month/quarter. When
coordinating multi-platform publishing.

## Input
Content pillars, publishing frequency, seasonal events, available content.

## Output
A content calendar with topics, formats, platforms, dates, and KPIs.

## Steps
1. Define content pillars: 3-5 core topics that define your brand (e.g., Big Data, AI, Profitability, Brand)
2. Set publishing cadence: blog (2x/week), X (daily), LinkedIn (3x/week), email (weekly)
3. Plan 4-6 weeks ahead: topics, working titles, target keywords, platform adaptations
4. Align with events: conferences, product launches, seasonal trends, holidays
5. Mix content types: how-to (40%), thought leadership (30%), news/trends (20%), personal (10%)
6. Assign ownership: who writes, who edits, who publishes per piece
7. Review weekly: adjust based on performance, news, and opportunities

## Rules
- Plan 4+ weeks ahead — last-minute content is lower quality
- Content pillars enforce focus: every piece should map to a pillar
- 40/30/20/10 mix: how-to, thought leadership, news, personal
- Maintain a content backlog: 10+ ideas ready for development
- Track content performance and adjust calendar based on data
- Coordinate with Lucas (strategy) and multi-platform-publish (execution)
