# Brand Audit

Conduct comprehensive brand audits — reviewing all touchpoints (web, social,
print, content) for visual and voice consistency — identifying gaps and
producing prioritized remediation plans.

## What problem this solves
Brands drift. A color changes here, a font there, a logo version gets stale —
cumulatively, the brand becomes unrecognizable. Regular audits catch drift
early and keep all properties aligned with canonical guidelines.

## When to use
Quarterly. After rebranding. When launching new properties. When visual or
voice inconsistency is noticed.

## Input
Canonical brand guidelines, list of properties to audit, audit scope.

## Output
A brand audit report with property-by-property findings, severity ratings,
and prioritized remediation actions.

## Steps
1. Load canonical guidelines from my-brands-guidelines
2. Inventory all touchpoints: websites, social profiles, presentations, documents
3. Audit each touchpoint against:
   - Visual: logo, colors, fonts, spacing, imagery
   - Voice: tone, terminology, copyright, taglines
   - Technical: favicon, Open Graph tags, structured data
4. Rate each finding by severity and produce fix instructions
5. Prioritize: critical (brand identity broken) → high → medium → low
6. Produce remediation plan with specific changes and effort estimates
7. Track fixes and re-audit to confirm compliance

## Rules
- Audit all touchpoints, not just websites — social media, slides, documents
- Canonical guidelines are the standard — compare to them, not to opinion
- Every finding needs a specific fix instruction — "colors are wrong" is not actionable
- Critical findings: logo missing/wrong, primary color incorrect, no copyright
- Quarterly audits minimum — more frequent for rapidly changing properties
- After fixes, re-audit — verify remediation, don't assume it happened
- Coordinate with Dorcas (brand governance) and visual-identity (guidelines)
