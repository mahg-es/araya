# KPI Framework

Design cascading KPI frameworks — strategic, tactical, and operational metrics
with leading/lagging indicators — aligning measurement from boardroom to
frontline operations.

## What problem this solves
Random metrics create confusion ("we're measuring 50 things but don't know if
we're winning"). A KPI framework cascades from strategy to execution: each
level answers a specific question with 3-5 focused metrics that drive action.

## When to use
When defining business metrics. When the organization has too many unaligned
KPIs. When building executive dashboards. When The Data Professor asks "how do
we measure success?"

## Input
Business strategy, objectives, operational processes, existing metrics.

## Output
A cascading KPI framework with strategic → tactical → operational metrics,
definitions, targets, and leading/lagging indicator balance.

## Steps
1. Define strategic objectives (3-5): what does success look like at the top?
2. Cascade to tactical KPIs per function: what does each team measure?
3. Cascade to operational KPIs: what do daily operations track?
4. Balance leading (predictive) and lagging (historical) indicators
5. Define each KPI: name, formula, data source, target, owner, review frequency
6. Limit to 3-5 KPIs per level — more dilutes focus
7. Implement dashboards and regular review cadence

## Rules
- Strategic KPIs: 3-5 total — if everything is a priority, nothing is
- Leading indicators predict the future; lagging indicators confirm the past
- Every KPI needs: definition, formula, data source, target, owner
- KPI tree: strategic → tactical → operational — cascading, not random
- Red/Amber/Green thresholds defined for every metric
- Review frequency: strategic (monthly), tactical (weekly), operational (daily)
- Coordinate with Pablo (dashboards) and data-visualization (charts)
