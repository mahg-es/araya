# Analytics Report

Generate executive and operational analytics reports — structured analysis
with insights, visualizations, and actionable recommendations — transforming
raw data into business decisions.

## What problem this solves
Data without analysis is noise. Stakeholders need conclusions, not raw data.
This skill produces polished analytics reports that answer three questions:
what happened, why did it happen, and what should we do about it?

## When to use
When stakeholders need data-driven insights. For monthly/quarterly business
reviews. When analyzing a specific business problem. When The Data Professor
asks "what does the data say?"

## Input
Data to analyze, business questions, audience, report format.

## Output
A structured analytics report with executive summary, analysis, visualizations,
insights, and recommendations.

## Steps
1. Define the business question: what decision does this report enable?
2. Gather and validate data — note data sources and quality
3. Analyze: trends, patterns, anomalies, comparisons, correlations
4. Visualize key findings with appropriate charts (use data-visualization skill)
5. Structure the report: Executive Summary → Analysis → Visualizations → Insights → Recommendations
6. Write insights, not just observations: "Revenue grew 15% because of X" not "Revenue went up"
7. Make recommendations specific and actionable: "Increase X budget by $Y to capture Z opportunity"

## Rules
- Executive Summary on page 1 — busy stakeholders may read nothing else
- Every chart must have a written insight — don't make readers interpret
- Recommendations must be specific: what, how much, by when, expected impact
- Data sources cited — stakeholders must trust the numbers
- Consistent format issue to issue — stakeholders should know where to look
- Note data limitations and assumptions — be honest about uncertainty
- Coordinate with Pablo (overall BI) and data-visualization (charts)
